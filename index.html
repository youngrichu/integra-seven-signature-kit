How to Set Up Your Signature
Applies to Outlook on the web (outlook.office.com) and the new Outlook desktop app for Windows. Both share the same settings interface, and signatures sync between them automatically (a restart of the desktop app may be needed to pull in changes made on the web).
1

Copy your signature

Click the Copy Signature button on your card above. This copies the fully rendered signature to your clipboard.

2

Open Settings

Click the gear icon in the top-right corner of Outlook to open the settings panel.

3

Go to Account > Signatures

In the settings panel, click Account in the left column, then click Signatures. Your existing signatures are listed here.

4

Add a new signature

Click the + Add signature button at the top right. Give it a name, e.g. Integra Seven.

5

Paste your signature

Click inside the editor area below the name field. Press Ctrl+A to clear any existing content, then Ctrl+V to paste. The logo and all formatting will render immediately.

6

Set as default and save

Use the Default for new messages and Default for replies and forwards dropdowns at the top of the Signatures page to assign your signature. Click Save.

Tip: You can manage multiple signatures from the same Signatures page. Outlook Web now lets you set different default signatures for new messages vs. replies and forwards, so you can use the full signature for new emails and a shorter one for replies.
Applies to Classic Outlook for Windows — the version with a ribbon toolbar and a File menu in the top-left corner. Its signature store is separate from Outlook Web and must be set up independently.
1

Copy your signature

Click the Copy Signature button on your card above.

2

Open the Signatures dialog

Go to File > Options > Mail > Signatures... Alternatively, open a new email, click the Insert tab in the ribbon, then click Signature > Signatures...

3

Create a new signature

Click New, type a name such as Integra Seven, and click OK. The new entry will appear in the list.

4

Paste into the editor

Click inside the Edit signature area at the bottom of the dialog. Press Ctrl+A to clear it, then Ctrl+V to paste. The logo and formatting will render in the editor.

5

Assign as default and click OK

In the Choose default signature area (top right of the dialog), select your email account and assign this signature to New messages and optionally Replies/Forwards. Click OK.

Classic vs New Outlook: Not sure which version you have? If you see a File menu in the top-left corner and a ribbon toolbar, you are on Classic. The new Outlook desktop app looks and works like the web version. They do not share signatures, so you need to set up separately in each if you use both.
Apple Mail does not support native HTML signature import. The most reliable approach is copying the rendered signature from Safari — other browsers may strip the formatting on paste, causing the logo and layout to break.
1

Open this file in Safari

If you are currently in Chrome or Firefox, copy the full URL from the address bar (it starts with file:///) and paste it into Safari's address bar.

2

Copy your signature in Safari

Click the Copy Signature button on your card. Safari preserves the rich HTML formatting that Apple Mail requires for the logo and layout to display correctly.

3

Open Mail Settings

In Apple Mail, go to Mail > Settings in the menu bar (on macOS Monterey and older, it is called Preferences). Click the Signatures tab.

4

Create a new signature

Select your email account in the left column, then click the + button below the middle column. A new signature entry with a default placeholder will appear. Give it a name.

5

Uncheck "Always match my default message font"

This checkbox appears below the signatures list. Make sure it is unchecked before pasting. If it is on, Mail will override the signature's typography with your default compose font.

6

Paste your signature

Click once inside the preview panel on the right side. Press Cmd+A to select the placeholder text, then Cmd+V to paste. The logo and formatted layout should appear.

7

Assign to your account and close

To make it the default, select your email account in the left column, then use the Choose Signature dropdown at the bottom of the panel to pick the new signature. Close Settings and you are done.

If the logo appears as a broken image: Apple Mail's privacy settings may be blocking external images. Go to Mail > Settings > Privacy and turn off Protect Mail Activity, then quit and reopen Mail. The logo is hosted on Cloudinary (a CDN) so it loads reliably once external content is allowed. You can re-enable the privacy setting after confirming the signature looks correct in a test email.
